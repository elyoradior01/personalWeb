BubblePath Application Prototype


   As part of the Apple Foundation Program, my team, Giber.Inc, was tasked with developing a problem-solving application prototype. Through careful discussion, we identified social media as a major source of problems and honed in on the issue of cyberbullying. We recognized that social media has both positive and negative impacts on its users, including children, who have access to technology and social media. With this in mind, we created BubblePath, an interactive and family-friendly game designed to educate children about the negative effects of cyberbullying.
   BubblePath is a role-playing game that allows children to select a character and choose from several family-friendly storylines. The game is designed to unconsciously teach children about respectful behavior and empathy through the storylines, which revolve around cyberbullying. The game has multiple potential plotlines, and the player's decisions determine the outcome of the story. The goal of the game is for children to develop empathy by practicing it in the game, learning not to cyberbully, and gaining the skills to handle cyberbullying situations.
   During this project, I was personally responsible for planning the concept, designing the application, creating content, and contributing to part of the coding.
